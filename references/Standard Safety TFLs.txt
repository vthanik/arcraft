General Points to Consider 
General Points to Consider (applies to all Tables, Listings and Graphs in this package)Column Ordering * The general principles for ordering treatment group columns is as follows:
* Columns are displayed in the order of dose escalation.
* Active treatment is first, and placebo/control is last.
o  In general, the active treatment should be placed in columns prior to the
 comparator/placebo arms, except for, e.g., dose-ranging or titration studies. 
* Total column for AE tables is optional. If included, the total should only include the active arms of the study, and not be a total of active and control/placebo.  If included, the total column should be placed immediately after the right-most active treatment column.Allowable Modifications* The recommended populations and subsets for the TLGs are seen in this document, but there are allowable modifications.  Allowable modifications are changes to the TLGs that can be made within the standard TLG macros. 
* The  modifications given below are allowed for all TLGs. In addition, there may be allowable modifications stated under the TLGs that applies directly to that TLG.
1.) Change of population
2.) Change in the treatment groups displayed including subtotal and total columns 
3.) Displaying a subset of events (for example adverse event of special interest or adverse event leading to discontinuation), provided the subsetting is indicated in the title or footnote.
4.) Change of title or footnote, provided the change properly reflects what is being displayed in the TLG.  
5.) May modify categorical variables as determined necessary by study team. For example, age categories may deviate from <65 and >=65 as long as the categories are inclusive of all subjects. 
6.) May modify the sort order of tables, be sure to specify sort order in footnotes. Dose Interruption * Dose interruption may be defined differently, depending on the drug/compound formulation (eg, oral  versus injectable), therefore may require  team  agreement.Footnote in TLG* Do not use the word "Note" at the beginning of the footnote section.
* Use alphabetic order such as [a], [b], ..., etc. and not numeric order such as [1], [2], ..., etc.Celgene convention* Use "Subject" instead of "Patient" in all TLGs.Treatment order* The treatments/treatment groups will be displayed as "XXXXX", "XXXXX" and "XXXXX". For the TLGs, unless otherwise specified, the treatments/treatment groups will appear from left to right (and top to bottom) in the order of "XXXXX", "XXXXX" and "XXXXX", as applicable.Spanning headers* While spanning headers may be necessary in some cases, it is recommended that they only be used when necessary. TLG not included in this package* The Table of Contents of this package only includes the most commonly used TLG for study reports (hence, "Standard"). 
* Variations of the TLG herein, such as subgroups by sex, age, time-period-of-interest, population-of-interest, etc., are recommended to use the same TLG formats to streamline the creations of the variations.Formatting issues* Use :  (xxx.x) , where the left and right parenthesis are in a consistent location in the column.  One hundred percent would include the decimal, i.e. (100.0).
* If the numerator is '0' then show '0'. If the denominator is '0', then show 'NA' - apply this logic for descriptive stats
* Do the rounding for the percentages before displaying in report.
* For tables that have a mix of continuous and categorical summaries (e.g. Demographics), the summary statistics should be centered
* Display as <= or >=, instead of = or =.
* Use superscripts to denote a value raised to a power (eg X2 instead of X^2).Tables, listings and graphs  numbering

For example, if your normal AE Summary table is 14.3.1.2 and you have two summary tables for the efficacy evaluable and safety populations, then they should be given the numbering 14.3.1.2.1 and 14.3.1.2.2.

Module #1 General Study Information Tables 
Table number (with brief description)Points to consider14.1.1. Screen Failures*  Consider adding basic demographic information (i.e., age, sex, race/ethnicity) to the beginning of the table. This will show the reviewer that a group of subjects were not systematically excluded which could bias results.14.1.3. Subject Disposition* This table shell is for the ITT/FAS population. If necessary, the team could run the same table for the safety population.14.1.5. Demographic and Baseline Disease Characteristics*  The Cutoff for age group may change depending on study (e.g., use >=70 instead of >=65)
*  Team may consider creating 3 separate tables from this single table:
o  Common general baseline demographics.
o  Disease specific demographics.
o  Baseline lab tests (determined by team).14.1.6. Protocol Violation*  This table contains only protocol violations, not deviations. 
*  Deviations can be provided in a separate listing, but are generally not provided in a separate table.14.1.7. Medical History*  This table should present only relevant medical history; the team should determine what qualifies as "relevant," depending on the relevant subject population, nature of disease, and expected EOIs, etc.14.1.9. Concomitant Medications* Unless otherwise specified, the Conmed  tables will be displayed in alphabetical order for the ATC1 Classification, and within each ATC1 Classification, the preferred names will be displayed in alphabetical order.14.3.1.1.1 Summary of Dose Information by Treatment Group* A uniform definition of treatment duration is needed, and should be specified in the SAP
o  Last dose + x days for compounds with long half-lives, versus last dose + 0 days for compounds with short half-lives as determined by study team.
