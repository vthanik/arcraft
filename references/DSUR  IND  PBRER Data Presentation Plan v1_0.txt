Introduction
Guidance for DPP Users and Reviewers
The Analysis Data Presentation Plan (DPP) contains comprehensive specifications for the development of data displays for an analysis. 
Templates and Metadata Tables:
Within the Analysis DPP, the following templates will reside:
Global Standard Templates (GS): The layout of these templates is a Global Standard. Global Standard templates will be placed into the DPP without modifications. 
Project Specific Templates (PS): The layout of these templates is constructed and described in the Core DPP. Project Specific templates will be placed into the DPP without modifications.
For Early Development Only - Model Document Specific Template (MS): The layout of these templates is constructed and described in the Early Development Standardized DPP Template.
Study SpecificTemplates (SS): The layout of these templates is constructed for and described in the Analysis DPP. 
A Metadata Table will contain information on the exact layout for a table, listing or graph.
DPP Conventions:
Text presented within a template and highlighted in grey:
is a generic indicator for the type of endpoint or phrase that should replace it (Measure or Period for example) 
represents an optional item for a particular template presentation (P-value or Tablets/Capsules, for example). 
The highlighted text in grey will remain as such in the template and the appropriate endpoint or phrase and the specifications to include within a specific table or listing in lieu of the highlighted text in grey will be described in the metadata tables. This will allow multiple tables to refer to the same table template.

DSUR
Guidance for generating DSUR:
Table 4A:
All previously completed clinical trials and ongoing and newly completed clinical trials for the drug under the reporting period needs to be included
For blinded studies or open-label studies but treated as blinded, the subject exposure should be based on blinded data (i.e. dummy treatment assignments based on protocol defined randomization schema) until the studies are unblinded
DO NOT count a study into multiple indication categories, eg, if a study has more than one indication, classify this study into ‘OTHER OR MULTIPLE INDICATION’ category.
For studies with crossover design (i.e. crossover from non-BMS drug arm to BMS drug arm), crossover subjects should be presented in both “BMS Drug” and “Comparator Treatment Arms”. In ‘Total Study Exposure to Any Treatment Arm’, crossover subjects will be only counted once. 
In special cases where a study has crossover design from BMS drug A (Control Arm) to BMS drug combo drug A + drug B (Experimental Arm), if drug B is under current reporting period, the same rule will be applied. However, if drug A is under current reporting period and this study was included in the study list, the following rule should be applied: both arms will be presented under ‘BMS Drug’; subjects assigned to BMS drug A will be allocated to ‘Group 1’ as ‘Mono therapy’ and subjects assigned to BMS drug A+B will be allocated to ‘Group 2’ as ‘Combo therapy’.
Table 4B and 4B-pediactric:
Number of subjects in ‘Total’ from Table 4B  should be equal to ‘Total’ under BMS Drug section from Table 4A
Only generate Table 4B-pediatric if more than one studies have pediatric population exposed.
EU Region-Specific Information for the Annual Safety Report (ASR)
EU region requires “List of subjects who died during the reporting period” and “List of subjects who dropped out of clinical tirals asscoiation with an AE during the reporting period” in the ASR. See Guidance #5 in IND section on bringing such information into DSUR.

IND
Guidance for generating IND tables:
IND tables only generate for ongoing studies.
Table nubering of outputs for ongoing studies (i.e. TEAE, treatment emergent SAE, AE leading to drug discontinuation, death):
“Table 1.FULLSTUDYNUMBER” will be used as Table No of “Treatment-emergent Adverse Event Identifier Summary”. 
“Table 2.FULLSTUDYNUMBER” will be used as Table No of “Treatment-emergent Serious Adverse Event Identifier Summary”.
“Table 3.FULLSTUDYNUMBER” will be used as Table No of “Listing of Subjects with Treatment Emergent Adverse Events Leading to Discontinuation of Study Medication”.
“Table 4.FULLSTUDYNUMBER” will be used as Table No of “Death Listing”.
For blinded studies, only ‘Total’ will be presented.
The final deliverables to IND reporting team should combine all ongoing studies for each unique table into one file. i.e. total 4 files to be submitted to PRISM.
EU Region-specific information requires “list of subjects who died during the reporting period” and “list of subjects who dropped out of clinical trials in association with an AE during the reporting period” in the ASR. Therefore, Table 3 and Table 4 listed above will be genereted for such prupose based on study list to be included in DSUR if DSUR and IND reports do not occur concurrently. If DSUR and IND report occur concurrently, Table 3 and Table 4 will be generated based on the maximum of the study list (i.e. union of the two study list sets) and served for both purposes (i.e. IND requirement and EU region-specific information requirement in ASR).
